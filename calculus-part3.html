🔗 Chain Rule Combos

The chain rule is the most used rule in calculus. Master it in combination with product rule, quotient rule, trig, exp, and log. These are layered — identify all the rules before you start.
Chain Rule: d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) · g'(x)
Work OUTSIDE IN. Each layer needs its own derivative multiplied. For triple chains: d/dx[f(g(h(x)))] = f'(g(h(x))) · g'(h(x)) · h'(x)
